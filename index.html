Product Development Project: Can Crusher

This project is a collaborative project between students who form a group that produces Can Crusher Products which is an innovation created from a simple form of can crusher. Innovation is carried out to meet market needs consisting of can recycling factories and canned beverage companies that provide vending machines. Can crushers are made to minimise the place to dispose of used beverage can waste and make it easier for collectors to recycle beverage can waste. I designed the product using the Autodesk Inventor application and we won the best product development in the sanitary category at an exhibition held by the Product Development Laboratory.

Final Projects Production Systems and Automation

In this project made with a group of students who will discuss the "Packing process in a tea factory using a packaging line system". The packing process is a series of steps taken to package or wrap goods or products with the aim of protecting these goods, facilitating transportation, and maintaining their quality. Packing activities have the concept of ‘Packaging Sells What It Protects’ which means packaging sells what it protects. In other words, packaging is no longer a protector or container but must be able to sell the product it packs. The tea packing process is a programme to fill Papersack containing tea into boxes which will later be moved to the warehouse.

Final Projects Computer Simulation

In this project made with a group of students who will discuss queue simulation at INDOMARET PBB Bojongsoang in an area close to Telkom University Bandung with the aim of minimising queues and maximising the number of consumers who can be served. Using Flexsim software will make two models for comparison, namely the existing model and the alternative model.
